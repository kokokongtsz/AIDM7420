Human in Hong Kong

Date: 14 March 2024
Venue: Kowloon Bay


My colleagues always suspected that I had a girlfriend in Japan, given my frequent trips to Japan. Twice, sometimes even once a month, I would leave the office on a Friday evening and head straight to the airport. By midnight on Monday, I would touch down in Hong Kong and head straight to work. This has been my routine for over 10 years now.

It's not about a secret romance. It's about my deep love for Japanese culture and the need to find solace and relaxation. Occasionally, I find myself attending J-pop star concerts, while other times I simply go with the flow and relax without any particular agenda. I've only been able to stay for 2-3 days most of the time due to limited annual leave.

When the COVID-19 pandemic hit, the feeling of being trapped in Hong Kong became unbearable. I could no longer bear the repetitive routine. So, I decided to "naked resign" (leaving the previous jobs before securing a new offer) from my job and embarked on a six-month adventure in Canada and Japan.

As an engineer by profession, my job provided a means of living and a sense of satisfaction. But I realized that there was so much more to life than just work. I have delved into various new experiences, such as learning French, practicing woodwork, and most recently, exploring the realm of saxophone playing.

I discovered that fulfillment came not only from a successful job but also from pursuing diverse interests. In Hong Kong, the common belief is that work is the ultimate pursuit. It's the prevalent value ingrained in society. But I came to understand that there is so much more to life. I chose to prioritize my personal growth and exploration in diverse fields, instead of solely concentrating on my career.
